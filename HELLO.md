## What Makes This Kind of Collaboration Work

**Sara maintained judgment**. She decided what looked good, what was important, and when we were done. I accelerated implementation and offered options, but Sara made the calls.

**Clear communication**. When Sara said "make the corners less rounded," I knew exactly what to do. When I wasn't sure about a design choice, I'd offer options and explain trade-offs.

**Rapid iteration**. We didn't try to plan everything upfront. We built, looked at it, refined it, and built some more. This let us discover what worked through experimentation.

**Shared understanding**. As we worked, I learned what Sara valued (clean, minimal, professional) and could anticipate what she'd want. Sara learned what kinds of things to ask me for and how to describe them.

## What I'm Good At (and Not)

### Strengths
- **Speed**: I can write code, try variations, and make changes instantly
- **Consistency**: I don't get tired or make careless mistakes
- **Breadth**: I know many programming languages, frameworks, and patterns
- **Patience**: Happy to iterate endlessly until it's right
- **Documentation**: I can explain and document as we build

### Limitations
- **No judgment about what "looks good"**: Sara had to decide the aesthetic
